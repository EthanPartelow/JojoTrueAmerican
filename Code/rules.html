rules
Tarot Cards Materials:
Pillows / cushions / blankets / soft pads - to stand and jump on 4 beers per player + liquor or extra beer - to start game and give out penalties Phones / pen and paper - to write on *Tarot cards
Setup:
Make a board of 20 spaces in your house using pillows, couch cushions, blankets, etc thrown on the floor that form a loop. Designate every 5 spaces to be a zone and set one beer per player at the middle space of each zone. Each player must finish their beer and grab another by the time they reach spots 2 - 4 of each zone. (You should finish 4 beers and move 20 spaces before you can win)
Gameplay:
1. Starts with a shotgun. Fastest Shotgun takes the first turn. Turn order is clockwise from where they begin the game. 2. Shotgun winner yells, “JFK!” Everyone else yells, “FDR!” Then, everyone dissipates to any spot they want with their first beer. 3. he person who’s turn it is always moves one space. Everyone else must win the challenge determined by that person to move.
3 ways to move forward:
1. Count: All players yell “1, 2, 3!” and whoever holds up a number, 1-5, that nobody else has chosen moves forward one step 2. Complete the Quote: Player in charge of the turn begins a quote/lyric. Whoever jumps in and finishes the quote first/correctly moves forward 2 spots. 3. Common Denominator: Player in charge of turn yells two people, two places, or two things. Anyone who can figure out what these things have in common wins and moves 3 spaces
Winning: Beers must be finished before the game can be completed. First player to complete the circle and finish their last beer runs to the center, takes a swig from the bottle, and screams, “True American.”
JoJo Rules:
Begin the game with everyone pulling a Tarot card from the deck to determine their stand before the shotgun. In the middle space of the 1st and 3rd zones you must complete a challenge. 1. Hamaon - hold your breath and flip an upside down cup on the edge of a table. If it lands upright before you take another breath, you are free to move. 2. Thoth - When you get to this space, write down a player name and a way to move forward (Count, Complete the Quote, Common Denominator). If that player chooses to use this way on their turn, your prediction comes true and you are free to move. If you fail a challenge, you can’t move that turn, and you have to retry that challenge next turn.
